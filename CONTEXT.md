# IdeaCourt

IdeaCourt helps founders pressure-test startup ideas before committing to product work.

## Language

**Viability Verdict**:
A decision on whether a startup idea should move forward, change direction, or stop for now. The verdict is one of **Build**, **Pivot**, or **Do Not Build Yet**.
_Avoid_: score, rating, vibe check

**Evidence Gate**:
The research checkpoint that must happen before startup-building artifacts are created. It uses market and customer evidence to inform the Viability Verdict.
_Avoid_: precheck, loading step, research phase

**Evidence Gate Dimension**:
One of the six lenses used to judge a startup idea before product planning: **Pain Urgency**, **Willingness To Pay**, **Market Pull**, **Competitive Opening**, **Reachable Customer**, and **MVP Feasibility**.
_Avoid_: metric, factor, category

**Evidence Rating**:
A qualitative judgment for an Evidence Gate Dimension. The rating is **Strong**, **Mixed**, or **Weak**, and must include reasoning grounded in evidence.
_Avoid_: numeric score, grade, percentage

**Source-Backed Reasoning**:
The explanation attached to an Evidence Rating. It cites available source evidence, labels confidence, and calls out assumptions instead of hiding them.
_Avoid_: opinion, vibe, hallucinated citation

**Pain Urgency**:
The Evidence Gate Dimension that judges whether the problem is painful and time-sensitive enough to motivate action.
_Avoid_: interest, annoyance, preference

**Willingness To Pay**:
The Evidence Gate Dimension that judges whether buyers already spend money, time, or serious effort around the problem.
_Avoid_: monetization vibes, pricing guess

**Market Pull**:
The Evidence Gate Dimension that judges whether external behavior, trends, or adoption patterns are moving in favor of the idea.
_Avoid_: hype, trendiness

**Competitive Opening**:
The Evidence Gate Dimension that judges whether the market leaves a credible wedge for a new entrant.
_Avoid_: no competitors, blue ocean

**Reachable Customer**:
The Evidence Gate Dimension that judges whether the founder can realistically find, contact, and convert early users.
_Avoid_: target audience, everyone, users

**MVP Feasibility**:
The Evidence Gate Dimension that judges whether a small founding team can ship a credible first version quickly enough to validate demand.
_Avoid_: technical possibility, full product feasibility

**Conditional Dossier**:
The startup output whose contents depend on the Viability Verdict. A Build verdict receives the full dossier; Pivot and Do Not Build Yet receive critique-first outputs instead of polished product artifacts.
_Avoid_: report, deck, generated plan

**Hard Gating**:
The rule that non-Build verdicts prevent full product-planning artifacts from being generated. Pivot and Do Not Build Yet outputs stay critique-first so weak ideas do not gain false credibility through polish.
_Avoid_: warning, soft recommendation, optional caution

**Build**:
A Viability Verdict meaning the evidence supports moving into product, finance, GTM, and UX planning.
_Avoid_: approved, guaranteed, winner

**Pivot**:
A Viability Verdict meaning the research found a real pain or opportunity, but the current idea angle is not strong enough as stated.
_Avoid_: maybe, tweak, partial yes

**Do Not Build Yet**:
A Viability Verdict meaning the evidence is too weak, too crowded, too low-value, or too uncertain to justify product planning.
_Avoid_: failed, bad idea, rejected

## Example Dialogue

Founder: "I want the agents to create the whole startup for this idea."

Domain expert: "First we need an Evidence Gate. It produces a Viability Verdict. With Hard Gating, if the verdict is Build, we continue into the full Conditional Dossier. If it is Pivot or Do Not Build Yet, we show the critique and next validation moves instead of making the idea look more mature than it is."
